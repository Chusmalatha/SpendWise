# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User
Our core users are **Engineering Managers**, **CTOs**, and **Founders** at Seed to Series B startups. These individuals manage teams of 10-100 people and are increasingly concerned about "AI sprawl"—the rapid, uncoordinated adoption of overlapping AI tools like ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, Cursor, and GitHub Copilot.

## Distribution Channels
- **Hacker News (YCombinator)**: Target "Show HN" to engage the early adopter developer community.
- **Subreddits**: r/SaaS, r/startups, and r/devops where burn rate optimization is a frequent topic.
- **Twitter/X**: Engage with the #buildinpublic and #AI communities.
- **Direct Outreach**: Targeted LinkedIn outreach to CTOs of companies with 20-100 employees.

## Initial Launch Sequence
1. **Product Hunt Launch**: Position SpendWise as a "Free AI Stack Auditor." The value prop "Find out if you're burning money on AI in 2 minutes" is designed for high CTR.
2. **Viral Loop**: Each audit result is shareable. We expect founders to share their "Efficiency Score" in founder Slack/WhatsApp groups.
3. **LinkedIn Content**: Share aggregate (anonymized) data about the "Average AI Waste" found across startups to establish thought leadership.

## Unfair Advantage
The SpendWise engine isn't just a calculator; it's a bridge to fulfillment. While generic SaaS management tools only provide visibility, SpendWise integrates with the **Credex Marketplace**. We don't just tell users they are overspending; we provide a one-click path to purchasing discounted credits that immediately reduce that spend.

## Success Metrics (Week 1)
- **Total Audits**: 500+
- **Lead Capture Rate**: >15%
- **Consultation/Marketplace Intent**: 5+ high-value teams (>50 seats).
- **Viral Coefficient**: >0.2 (measured by traffic to `/results/public/:id` URLs from unique referrers).
